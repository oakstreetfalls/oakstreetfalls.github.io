Sorry I took so long to get this out. The spreadsheet has three sections. In all cases, it calculates the volume of the bay to be pressurized, then you either input the desired nose cone force, desired pressure change, or grams of powder. In each case it calculates the other two - e.g. when inputting NC force, it calculates pressure change and grams of powder. It also calculates how many shear pins will be reliably sheared using that amount of powder, based on the shear force required per pin and the total force on the bulkhead. For 4" rockets, I typically target 150 lb force, which will easily shear 3 2-56 nylon screws. You will see I put a suggested starting point on the sheet, listed as JC Main and JC drogue. Unless the bay is packed very full, I usually do not make much of an adjustment to the volume for recovery components, and I always ground test.

 

Let me know if you have any questions.
